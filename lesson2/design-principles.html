Design Principles

Visual Hierarchy

Organization/Company: Spotify

Website: www.spotify.com

Description: The website for Spotify has a clear visual hierarchy to highlight its most important elements. The website's various sections are highlighted through the use of big, strong headlines and bright pictures. Users can quickly identify between headers, subheadings, and body text because to clear colour contrasts and uniform font. Spotify effectively directs customers around its website by using visual hierarchy, highlighting the most pertinent information and enticing them to explore its music streaming options.

Hick's Law

Organization/Company: Apple

Website: www.apple.com

Description: Apple's website exhibits a straightforward and narrowly focused interface in accordance with Hick's Law. Less alternatives and goods are featured on the front page, which eases customers' cognitive strain. Apple makes it simpler for users to navigate and choose their products by offering a clean and straightforward set of options.

Rule of Thirds

Organization/Company: Nike

Website: www.nike.com

Description: The Rule of Thirds is used in the design of the Nike website to produce visually appealing and balanced compositions. You'll see that on the homepage, important components like product photos, headlines, and calls to action are deftly positioned along the grid lines. By establishing a sense of harmony and balance, this helps users focus on key areas of the page. Nike effectively engages customers through showcasing products, communicating brand messages, and using the Rule of Thirds.
